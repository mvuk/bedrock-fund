This report applies the Markowitz mean-variance optimization framework to your selected assets. Starting from raw daily prices, we computed returns, estimated the joint distribution of all assets (mean vector and covariance matrix), then solved for the set of portfolios that offer the best possible risk-return tradeoffs.

Frontier Parameters

These five constants fully characterize the efficient frontier. They are derived from the inverse of the covariance matrix and encode all the information needed to find optimal portfolios. A and C relate to how the assets combine, B measures total available return, D controls the curvature of the frontier, and H measures the maximum reward per unit of risk when a risk-free asset is available.

Portfolio Comparison

Three special portfolios emerge from the optimization. The Minimum Variance Portfolio (MVP) has the lowest possible risk of any combination. The Market Portfolio (tangency) offers the highest reward-to-risk ratio. The Zero-Correlation Portfolio has zero statistical dependence with the Market Portfolio - its return equals the risk-free rate, confirming a key theoretical prediction.

Graph 1 - Efficient Portfolio Frontier

This graph is the visual summary of the entire analysis. The curved line is the Efficient Portfolio Frontier - the boundary of all achievable risk-return combinations. The MVP (leftmost point) is where risk is minimized. The Market Portfolio is where a line from the risk-free rate touches the frontier tangentially - this is the optimal risky portfolio. The straight line (CML) from the risk-free rate through the Market Portfolio represents the best available tradeoff when you can mix risk-free and risky assets. The ZC Portfolio has zero correlation with the Market Portfolio and sits on the lower (inefficient) branch.

These tables show how each portfolio allocates across your assets. The MVP concentrates in low-volatility defensive assets. The Market Portfolio takes leveraged long/short positions to maximize the Sharpe ratio - this is mathematically optimal but not practically implementable without margin accounts.

The Bedrock Fund IS the Market Portfolio - a fixed set of weights across our 20 assets, optimized to maximize the Sharpe ratio. As fund managers, we built this portfolio using the Markowitz framework. As an investor, your only decision is how much of your capital goes into our fund versus a risk-free asset like Treasury bills. This is Tobin's Two-Fund Separation: we handle the asset allocation, you handle the risk level.
